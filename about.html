About ITCC

Overview

IT Case Challenge (ITCC) Competition made its successful inauguration in 2017 and received strong commendation from institutions of higher learning and industry experts as an excellent dais to assess students' cross functional knowledge, primarily in the iT field and other related fields.

The aim of this competition is to help students discover their innovative minds and problem solving skills by unraveling case studies specifically constructed by leading companies and industry specialists.

ITCC will be held over 2 days, where Day 1 students will be equipped with problem solving skills and given exposure on key inudstry trends through seminars and workshops. On Day 2, students deliberate on the case and present their recommendations to a panel of judges.

Categories

Category 1 – Institutions of Higher Learning (IHLs)
Each IHL can send ONE (1) group of THREE students (Diploma and/or Degree students). Each group will be accompanied by ONE (1) Coach.
Category 2 – Secondary Schools (Public and Private/International)
Each school can send ONE (1) group of THREE students. Each group will be accompanied by ONE (1) Coach.

NOTE: For respective categories, all participants must be registered as a student of the institute of higher learning or secondary school. Coach must be a selected academician from the same institute or secondary school and must be present during the entire 2 days of ITCC.

How the Competition Works?

Round 1

On Day 1, each group will be given a case study specifically constructed by leading companies and industry specialists. Focus areas include (but not limited to) Internet of Things (IoT), Big Data, Social Media, Industry 4.0 and Information Science. At the end of Day 1, students can begin deliberating the case and propose appropriate solutions for the problems outlined in the case.

On Day 2, each group will present their solution (case presentation) to a panel of judges.

Round 2

After the case presentation, top 3 groups for each category will be announced. These groups will be given more time for deliberation, then required to present their solutions again during a final debate. As a result of this debate, winners will be announced.
